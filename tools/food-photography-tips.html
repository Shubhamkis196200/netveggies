Natural Window Light

Shoot near a large window during daytime. Soft, diffused light is best â€” avoid direct harsh sunlight.

Golden Hour

1-2 hours after sunrise or before sunset gives warm, flattering light. Perfect for mood shots.

Built-in flash creates harsh shadows and unappetizing colors. Natural light always wins.

  • Exposure slider: Swipe up/down after tapping to adjust brightness
  • Portrait mode: Blurs background beautifully (use on iPhone/modern Androids)
  • Grid lines: Turn on in settings for composition help
  • Never zoom digitally: Move closer instead â€” zoom reduces quality
  • Clean your lens! Wipe with soft cloth before shooting
  • Shoot in natural light mode: Avoid "food" filters â€” edit later

âœï¸ Editing Basics

Brightness & Contrast

Brighten slightly, boost contrast a bit. Food should look fresh and appetizing, not washed out.

Warmth

Add a touch of warmth (yellow/orange tones). Makes food look more delicious and inviting.

Saturation (Careful!)

Boost vibrant colors (greens, reds) but don't overdo it. Keep skin tones and neutrals natural.

Shadows & Highlights

Lift shadows slightly to reveal detail. Pull down highlights if food looks blown out.

Free Editing Apps: Snapseed (Google), Lightroom Mobile (Adobe), VSCO, or built-in iPhone Photos editor
